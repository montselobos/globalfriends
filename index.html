Global Friends

A digital experience for organizing your international friendships

This is a design excercise by Montse Lobos as part of Fino's recruitment process for UX designer

The task:

Create a mobile app for classifying your friendships.

1. Understanding the problem

To understand the problem space I conducted individual interviews with 5 participants whose ages ranged between 22 and 30 years old.

The interview centered around which distinctions they make between their friends, how they currently group them or categorize them and for which purposes.

Some interesting findings of this exercise were:

People use both 'categories' and 'groups' to organize their friends:

Some friends may share a category (women in tech) but they're not part of a 'group' (high school friends)

People in groups are interconnected

Group members know each other and are friends in most cases.

Subgroups

Each distinct group of friends has different sub groups based on emotional closeness or interests in common

Functional Subgroups

People use grouping to communicate to a specific audience

Outer Groups:

Each distinct group of friends has a group or 'friends by association' attached to it, made of friends of friends or partners of friends

Most of the participants I interviewed were international graduate students from either Columbia University or Carnegie Mellon University. Two of them were americans but had lived abroad for many years.
By talking to them I identified an interesting opportunity space regarding organizing friendships.

Most of these people struggle with language and location issues when posting or consuming content to/from their friends.

Example:

  • 'Sharing a joke that only makes sense in spanish feels rude'
  • 'I post things aimed to my Colombian friends and my english-speaker friends will use google translate and then comment on them, which is fine, but I mean, it was a very specific thing"
  • 'Sometimes I ask verbs specific college things and everyone back home likes them and it's weird'
    • 'Whenever I talk to someone back home my phone will autocorrect my spanish. I need to manually change the language preference each time I text someone"
  • 'Now that I'm in NY I don't care about everything that's going on back home, but I do want to know about any event going on in the city"

Some other common use cases were:

  • 'I am very interested in following career-related updates from some of my school mates, but I really don't care about their babies or dogs"
  • Grouping people to organize an event or for communicating for a determined period of time, like a group of friends attending the same conference.
  • Sending out invitations for birthday parties: selecting the list of invitees and sending the invites through the proper channel.

Persona

Ana Manzur

Ana is a 24-years-old international student in Columbia University. Originally from Colombia, It is very important for her to stay in touch with her family and friends, but without letting that become a distraction from her studies.

Just by moving to NY to join her masters program Ana expanded her friend base significantly and she is currently discovering what she has in common with each of them

Her main goal is to saty informed of academic and social events related to her new program, bond with her new friends in the city while keeping in touch with her 'back-home' friends as much as possible.

2. Goal and content strategy

GOAL

To organize friendships based on origin, language and current location in order to:
  1. Filter the notifications, updates and type of content I receive from them
  2. Filter the content I share with different groups and categories
  3. Make sure that my messages do not get mistakenly auto-corrected by my phone

CONTENT STRATEGY

Ana & Friends' Info

In order to achieve its goal, the app must have access the following information about Anna and her friends:

  • Where is she from
  • Languages she speaks
  • Where is she currently based?
  • Current Location
  • Where did she attended college, hisghschool, etc
  • Previous jobs
  • Virtual groups she's part of

Data Sources:

  • Facebook
  • LinkedIn
  • etc

Basic grouping can be done based on social media accounts' information. The user's input can perfect the groupings.

The system should be able to watch the user's behavior and learn from it. it should be able to identify:

  • Language settings when Anna chats with different friends
  • Mutual friends
  • New events or locations in common
